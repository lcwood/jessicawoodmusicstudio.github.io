Fostering musical minds and souls.

Jessica Wood Music Studio will make music your favorite part of the day!




AboutFun. Motivated. Passionate about music.



Jessica began playing piano at age 5 and has never stopped. At the age of 11 the violin also made its permanent appearance in her life.

She is committed to helping others reach and exceed their musical goals. Learn more about why Jessica may be the perfect piano/violin teacher for you!

Teaching Experience.

Jessica has been teaching piano and violin since 2007, but began seriously acquiring students and dedicating her time to teaching in 2014.

From 2014 through early 2018 Jessica taught at both the Lexington School of Music and Columbia Arts Academy located in Lexington, SC and Columbia, SC, respectively. She taught piano and violin to students from all musical backgrounds. This included children as young as 4 to mature adults of 83 years and had as many as 50 weekly students. In addition to private lessons, she conducted many group lessons for group sizes ranging from 2 to 10 students.

Jessica adjusts her teaching techniques to best fit the learning style of each individual and helps her students reach their goals with hands on musical examples and practice plans.

Musical Training.

Jessica holds a Music Minor in Music Arts with a concentration in Piano from Oral Roberts University, in Tulsa, OK (she received a Bachelor of Science degree in Biomedical Engineering in 2013 through ORU).

Piano:
Jessica studied piano with Joan Cosgrove of New Castle, PA for thirteen years. She also frequently participated in master classes and attended several Piano Workshops to further her skills.
During her undergrad years at Oral Roberts University, Jessica continued studying piano under Joyce Bridgman.

Violin:
Jessica studied under James Stephenson of New Castle, PA, for seven years. She also received violin instruction from a music intensive summer camp.

Noteable Achievements.

Piano:
As an undergrad, Jessica received a scholarship for winning a contemporary solo competition at Oral Roberts University.

Violin:
In her sophomore and junior years as an undergrad at Oral Roberts University, Jessica shared the Concert Master seat with seniors and became the sole Concert Master her senior year of study.

Piano:
In 2009, Jessica became the 3rd student in the state of Pennsylvania to earn the President’s Cup (the highest level of achievement) in Piano Solo through the National Federation of Music Clubs’ Pennsylvania Festivals. She had received thirteen consecutive Superiors in Piano Solo and eleven consecutive Superiors in Piano Concerto. Jessica also earned her Third Gold Cup in Piano Duets by receiving nine consecutive Superior ratings. Jessica was a participant of the National Guild of Piano Teacher’s Auditions for thirteen years.
Other accomplishments include being a frequent winner of the Youngstown State University/Music Teachers Association Master Piano Competition. At the age of 17 she received first place in the Pennsylvania Federation of Music Club’s Northwest District’s Aikens-Cadman Memorial Auditions, advancing to compete at the state level. She also qualified for showcase recitals of the Pennsylvania Music Teachers Association Dorothy Sutton Festivals and received third place in the Lois Streator/Lois Smith Memorial Award Competition in Pittsburgh.

Violin:
Jessica was a member of the Youngstown Symphony Youth Orchestra for five years and earned the position of Concert Master her senior year of high school.


Frequently Asked Questions
Find out more about lessons at Jessica Wood Music Studio.

Where are you located?

Jessica Wood Music Studio is located in my home studio in Ellwood City, PA (less than a mile from the Ellwood City Hospital).


What are your prices?

The lesson rates vary from $18 - $22 for a half hour and $32 - $40 for an hour. Contact the studio to see which price you qualify for!


How often do I have lessons?

Lessons are on a weekly basis with the same day and time slot for each week.


How/when do I make a payment?

Tuition is based on a month-to-month commitment and is due on (or before) the first lesson of each month. If the monthly tuition payment is not received by the 10th of the month, a $10.00 overdue fee will be added. Your first lesson is the only exception to this rule.
